# Gisela Jung CA Architects - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-10-21

---

## Summary and Insights

### Project Context and Feasibility Stage
The Mt Isa Research Centre feasibility study is progressing through a two-stage market validation process (0:30-1:17). A market demand deep dive conducted with SMI (Sustainable Minerals Institute at UQ) is scheduled for completion by mid-December, establishing a critical go/no-go decision point. The business case and scoping study have already been completed by SMI, with Maven brought in to validate market demand assumptions. Shane Kelly's team (Maven) was subsequently appointed to conduct the full feasibility study, and the project is currently in the stakeholder consultation phase (1:45-2:35).

### Strategic Purpose and Regional Capability Building
The Centre addresses a significant gap in regional technical capability (3:38-4:51). Currently, complex metallurgical work from the Mount Isa region must be sent interstate to Perth or Brisbane, or internationally to Sweden or China for analysis. The Research Centre aims to build sovereign capability by enabling this technical research to be undertaken locally in Mount Isa. The strategic positioning focuses on building regional capacity in critical minerals research and testing (14:56-15:19), which aligns with the recently announced US-Australia Critical Minerals deal worth $8.5 billion (14:30-14:47).

### Facility Design Concept
The facility will be structured as a large shed-based building with distinct functional zones organized by material flow (6:49-7:54). The design concept follows a "dirty to clean" workflow: raw rock samples enter at one end for crushing, progress through separation processes (flotation, magnetic, gravity, or leaching), produce concentrates that move to labs for testing, and ultimately flow through to piloting facilities. The design involves building structures within structures, with laboratories fabricated as enclosed spaces within the main shed, similar to cool rooms within a larger shelter (7:39-8:20). An administration and management building will sit at the front of the facility.

### Additional Centre Components
Beyond core research facilities, the Centre will include a learning and training component (5:00-5:36). This facility will provide in-house technical upskilling and professional development for mining executives and workers, bringing expert-led courses to Mount Isa rather than requiring regional personnel to travel for training. Shane is also proposing on-site accommodation consisting of 10-15 self-contained apartments or demountable units (13:24-14:16), modeled on a successful approach implemented at Mapoon Aboriginal Shire Council. This would facilitate easier access for researchers and visiting experts, though the feasibility depends on the local rental market dynamics, which may be affected by recent positive economic developments including the Glencore rescue package (14:30-16:04).

### Site Selection and Parameters
Several potential sites have been identified with council assistance (3:02-3:10), with the selected location expected to range between 2-5 hectares (11:31-11:38). The preferred sites are vacant commercial parcels on the edge of the industrial centre, still featuring existing trees, rather than locations further out of town (12:45-13:01). Amenity and accessibility for personnel are key considerations in site selection (13:01-13:24). There is discussion about potentially co-locating a quartz bagging and wash plant on the same site, though Shane expressed reservations about having such a dusty, dirty, and noisy operation directly adjacent to the Research Centre (11:38-12:26).

### Design Development Process and Timeline
The architectural scope is being structured in two phases (3:10-3:36, 10:57-11:09). Phase 1 will deliver site plan and floor plan drawings to support government funding applications, providing visual representation of the proposed facility. Phase 2 will produce three renders if the project proceeds. Shane is working with technical staff at UQ to finalize sizing requirements, which must account for both current needs and future expansion potential (8:30-8:49). The flow of materials through the facility is a critical design consideration, informed by visits to the JK Tech facility at UQ, which was built incrementally over time rather than as a purpose-designed integrated facility (9:49-10:39). Shane expects to provide Gisela Jung (CA Architects) with a comprehensive brief within a couple of weeks (9:19-9:29), and the overall project timeline extends to completion around March (17:52-17:57).

### Market Context and Strategic Positioning
The timing of the project aligns with significant developments in the critical minerals sector (14:30-15:19). The announcement of an $8.5 billion US-Australia Critical Minerals deal provides strong strategic context for positioning the Centre as a builder of sovereign capability in critical minerals research and testing. Combined with the secured Glencore rescue package, these developments may positively impact the regional economy and the Centre's strategic value proposition.

### Architect Selection and Next Steps
CA Architects was selected based on Tim's recommendation and their experience with research centres, including environmental research facilities for JCU (6:06-6:49). While they haven't worked specifically in the minerals field, Gisela confirmed interest in the project, particularly the early concept design phase (17:15-17:32). The next steps involve Shane finalizing technical requirements with UQ staff, providing a detailed brief including room schedules and spatial requirements, and potentially arranging for council to conduct a survey of the selected site (17:57-18:28). A budget has been established for the architectural work, though specific figures were not discussed (9:29-9:49, 21:04-21:16).
