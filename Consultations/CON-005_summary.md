# Matt Chauhan & Jordan Gooch - Sedgeman - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-10-23

---

## Summary and Insights

### Sedgeman Capabilities and Background
Matt Chauhan and Jordan Gooch outlined Sedgeman's unique position as an engineering-led EPC contractor with full vertical integration (4:01-8:16). Jordan emphasized their capability spans all SMP disciplines and that they maintain in-house design expertise rather than outsourcing, distinguishing them from construction-led EPC contractors. The company operates fluidly across EPC, EPCM, and client representation roles, with project sizes ranging from small works as low as $5,000 up to major complex projects like QR Cuff (8:30-9:58). Notably, David Wei, former CEO of JK Tech, now works at Sedgeman, providing additional technical knowledge connections (16:19).

### Mt Isa Research Centre Concept and Objectives
Shane outlined the evolution from an initial rare earth elements focus to a broader Critical Minerals Research Centre serving the Mount Isa region (10:01-11:27). The primary driver is addressing significant logistical challenges where even basic assays must be sent thousands of kilometers away, and more complex technical testwork currently requires overseas shipment to China, Sweden, Germany, and the US. Some minerals with contaminants cannot travel by air and must go by boat, creating substantial bottlenecks (11:27).

The Centre will span the full technical workflow from sample preparation through comminution, classification, metals extraction, bench-scale testing in wet and dry labs, culminating in a small pilot plant (17:34-18:13). Shane stressed the Centre is designed to complement rather than compete with other Queensland research facilities that operate larger-scale plants, fitting within the broader research architecture (18:13-18:20).

### Service Positioning and Scale Considerations
A critical insight emerged around the Centre's position in the development pipeline. Jordan clarified that QR Cuff operates at a higher scale, demonstrating commercially scalable circuits for vanadium, molybdenum, cobalt, and rare earths (18:24-19:45). The proposed Mt Isa Centre would fill the gap before QR Cuff's scale, providing essential lab-scale testing to prove flowsheets before financiers will fund full commercial facilities. Jordan noted that many QR Cuff stakeholders needed exactly this earlier-stage capability when they first approached the project (24:51-25:18).

Shane emphasized the Centre aims to help explorers understand extraction approaches for Critical Minerals in specific geological contexts early in their development, building on existing research about processing minerals in clays, ores, and various rock types (25:26-26:35).

### Technical Equipment Challenges and Batch Processing Solutions
Jordan shared valuable lessons from QR Cuff regarding pilot plant equipment limitations (20:43-21:52). Finding equipment small enough for pilot-scale operations proved technically challenging beyond just cost considerations. Specific bottlenecks included cyclones and rotary kilns that were not available at the required small scale. Sedgeman's solution involved designing batch processing approaches where certain unit operations run at 5-10 times the throughput capacity of the rest of the plant, operating quickly in bursts while remaining on standby otherwise.

### Hydrometallurgical Circuit as Core Value Proposition
Jordan identified the hydromet circuit as the critical value-adding stage warranting focus (21:57-24:14). Once ore is crushed and floated to produce a pre-roast concentrate, the value lies in the leaching and extraction process that produces the final precipitate product. The drying and calcining processes before and after are relatively straightforward. This insight suggests the Centre should prioritize controllable lab-scale hydromet capabilities, as equipment limitations primarily affect the crushing and drying stages that drive scale-up requirements.

Paul Holden's input that ore should not move too far led to discussion of the Centre potentially providing concentrates rather than processing full-scale material initially (22:15-22:36). This could optimize the relationship between lab-scale testing and larger pilot facilities.

### Commercial Viability and Assay Services
Shane stressed the Centre must operate commercially and pay its own way, not function purely as a research facility (27:31). A key commercial opportunity identified is providing independent assay testing for copper concentrates that Glencore will import to the Mount Isa smelter (26:44-27:09). Additionally, the Centre will provide essential assay capability to support its own research work and that of partners like JK Tech, who currently face delays when commercial labs prioritize high-volume clients over small research sample batches (26:35-26:44).

### Staged Development Approach
Shane indicated the Centre will consider staged implementation, recognizing that a pilot plant may not be required from day one (24:17-24:33). This phased approach aligns with building demand and proven capability before investing in larger-scale equipment, particularly given the equipment challenges Jordan identified.

### Next Steps and Collaboration
The project is in its consultation phase, expecting to complete stakeholder discussions within 2-3 weeks (16:19-17:06). Shane is refining the floor plan and service mix before commissioning technical feasibility work to determine operational costs for the proposed Centre capabilities (17:06-29:16). The technical feasibility was originally targeted to begin before Christmas but timing depends on properly defining the concept and finding the right partner. Shane expressed strong interest in Sedgeman potentially conducting this technical feasibility work given their QR Cuff experience and relevant expertise (28:18-30:08). Matt offered to connect Shane with explorers and junior miners who are currently sending work offshore and would benefit from local capabilities (30:19-31:00).
