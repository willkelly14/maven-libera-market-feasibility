# Kate Dickson - AMEC Stakeholder Consultation - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-11-06

---

## Summary and Insights
### Project Overview and Value Proposition
Shane Kelly explained that Mount Isa City Council initiated this project in response to Glencore flagging potential closure of operations in the region (1:42). Working with Critical Minerals Queensland and Paul Holden's team, the concept emerged to establish a critical minerals research institute and commercial advanced metallurgical test work center (2:20). The facility will focus on beneficiation, particularly around tailings reprocessing, vanadium, and other critical minerals, with emphasis on developing methods and flow sheets (2:48-3:20). A key differentiator is the inclusion of a learning center focused on critical minerals, providing lab skill qualifications and professional development opportunities (4:20-4:45).
### Relationship to Existing Infrastructure
When Kate Dickson asked about the QR Cuff facility in Townsville (5:06), Shane clarified that the Mt Isa center would complement rather than compete with existing infrastructure (5:23-6:08). Projects closer to Mount Isa could complete beneficiation locally and then truck concentrates to Townsville for piloting, rather than transporting ore across the entire distance. This would potentially increase utilization of the QR Cuff facility rather than cutting across it (5:52-6:08). Shane emphasized that Critical Minerals Queensland's involvement ensures facilities work together rather than compete (6:17-6:29).
### Commercial Positioning and Market Gap
Kate raised an important question about how the center would position itself relative to existing commercial suppliers like Brisbane Met Labs and SGS (6:33-6:59). Shane explained the facility will have commercial capability but won't be a full commercial supplier competing head-to-head (6:59-7:23). The key focus is building capability for advanced, complicated test work currently sent to China, Finland, Germany, or the US, essentially developing sovereign research capability in the region (7:23-7:50). Shane noted specific examples like high purity cobalt refinement work done in China, where clients sometimes question whether to trust the results (8:50-9:10). The center will also handle larger sample work for tailings reprocessing, which isn't practical to truck to Brisbane or Perth (9:20-9:49).
### Commodity Scope and Regional Focus
The center's scope extends beyond critical minerals alone. Shane explained that with Glencore sourcing third-party copper and most miners having critical minerals mixed with gold and copper, the facility needs capability across gold, copper, phosphates, zinc, and other commodities to avoid being too narrowly focused (12:14-13:10). The equipment required for advanced critical minerals work will support this broader range anyway (13:10-13:20). The geographic focus covers the Northwest Minerals Province and the eastern side of the Northern Territory—essentially anything east of Alice Springs and the Gulf region, where transport economics favor Mount Isa over Perth or South Australia (13:20-14:18).
### Potential Stakeholder Companies
Kate immediately identified several relevant companies for consultation. Mount Isa Minerals with their quartz deposits was mentioned first (10:21-10:42), which Shane confirmed was already on his list via contact with Nick Huffles, who expressed excitement about the facility (10:36-10:54). Additional companies identified included Aeon Metals with their Walford Creek project (10:54-11:00), Victory Metals with projects in the region (11:00-11:15), and Transition Minerals with rare earths and other interesting deposits (11:44-12:14). Kate also suggested Plutonic as worth investigating (14:18-14:34) and offered to consult with her AMEC colleague covering the Northern Territory for companies in that eastern region (13:39-14:02).
