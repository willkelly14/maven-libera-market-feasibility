# Ben Paxton-Hall Northwest Phosphate - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-11-06

---

## Summary and Insights

### Northwest Phosphate's Current Operations and Strategic Direction
Northwest Phosphate has commenced **direct shipping ore (DSO) operations** earlier in 2025, selling rock to local fertiliser companies as a small revenue driver (4:08-4:56). This operational phase runs parallel to their completion of a joint venture study to materialise phosphate rock concentrate for fertiliser, which will be primarily targeted at export markets (4:56-5:56).

The company is pursuing **research and development into animal feed applications** for their phosphate products, specifically looking at direct application and lipbox applications (3:16-3:25, 12:55-13:11). While this R&D initiative is described as a "longer-term thing" with planning and manufacturing still "a fair way off" (12:55-13:11), it represents a potential early research program for the Mt Isa Research Centre and could serve as an ideal initial major project to justify funding (3:25-4:08).

### Mount Isa Industrial Facility Development
Northwest Phosphate has secured a **lease over unallocated state land in Mount Isa City**, located very close to the Horizon loadout facility and adjacent to the rail line at the rodeo area (5:56-6:34). The site comprises three lots in an established industrial precinct and has room for four or five tenants, with a vision for a **multi-user facility** (6:24-6:34, 8:21-8:43).

A **development application (DA) is planned for submission in November 2025** (8:51-9:15). The site is flat, directly adjacent to railway infrastructure, and located in an industrial zone with no remnant vegetation issues, making it suitable for industrial development (9:43-10:08). The DA will include formal proposals with surveyed lot sizes and will address waste runoff and environmental considerations as part of the approvals process (9:15-9:43, 7:39-8:15).

Northwest Phosphate's intention is to **manufacture fertiliser at the facility**, and they maintain regular contact with Mount Isa City Council (Tim and team) regarding the development plans (5:56-6:03, 8:15-8:43).

### Research Centre Site Considerations and Alignment
The Mt Isa Research Centre feasibility has evolved to include **bulk minerals beneficiation capabilities**, which significantly changes the Centre's dynamics and site requirements (6:34-7:39). This addition means the Centre will need bulk sample holding areas, bulk concentrates holding areas, and crucially, facilities to manage "quite significant tailings waste" rather than minimal waste (6:34-7:39). This expanded scope is driving larger site area requirements and has influenced the technical feasibility planning process (10:06-11:39).

Despite initial concerns about tailings compatibility with the Northwest Phosphate site, Ben noted that given the industrial nature of the precinct and Mount Isa's context, waste management requirements could "be able to be accommodated" as part of their DA environmental studies (7:39-8:21). Shane agreed that a detailed briefing on Northwest Phosphate's proposed site layout would be beneficial to assess potential co-location opportunities (8:21-8:51).

### Timelines and Coordination
Shane is engaging a **technical feasibility expert** to develop the Research Centre site plan, with expectations to have a clear understanding of space requirements by **mid-December 2025** (10:06-11:39, 14:12-14:15). The feasibility study is targeted for completion **sometime in March 2026**, followed by a funding pursuit phase (15:22-16:01). Shane noted that dirt wouldn't likely be turned for "probably three years," positioning this as a long-term project (16:01-16:03).

A **site visit is planned for November 24-25, 2025**, with Shane flying up on the 23rd and returning on the 26th (11:47-12:11). Ben indicated he would also be in Mount Isa over the coming weeks, creating an opportunity for an in-person site walkthrough and review of development plans (11:39-11:47, 16:34-16:51).

### Funding Outlook and Support Requirements
Shane characterized the funding environment as significantly more favorable than five or six weeks prior, describing the project as "flavour of the month" (13:23-14:12). However, he acknowledged the Centre will require "a chunk of change" to implement (14:12-14:15).

To secure funding, Shane emphasized the critical importance of **firming up demand commitments** from potential users like Northwest Phosphate (14:15-15:17). Even pipeline projects three or four years out would be valuable, provided statements of intent confirm that companies would conduct research in Mount Isa if the lab and technical capabilities are available (14:15-15:17). Ben committed that Northwest Phosphate would be **"happy to provide any support"** for letters of intent or demand commitments (15:17-15:22).

### Site Location and Characteristics
The Research Centre site requirements remain flexible regarding location, with Shane noting it "can pretty much be located anywhere" that makes sense, though ultimately **Mount Isa City Council will determine the final location** (11:39-11:47). Shane received preliminary information from Ross about potential site options and planned to walk the Northwest Phosphate site during his November visit to better assess surface conditions and suitability (12:06-12:39).
