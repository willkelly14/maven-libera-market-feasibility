# David Cavanagh & Dylan Molver Core Resources - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-11-14

---

## Summary and Insights
**Project Background and Scope**
Shane outlined the project's evolution, explaining that approximately 12 months ago, the Sustainable Minerals Institute at UQ was engaged by Mount Isa City Council and Critical Minerals Queensland to assess the feasibility of a Rare Earths focused Research Centre (2:59-4:55). The project has now progressed to a full feasibility study phase, with SMI stepping back from this phase to avoid potential conflicts of interest as they may ultimately operate the facility. Shane's role brings a commercial advisory perspective to complement the technical expertise being brought in.

**Core Resources' Experience with Common User Facilities**
David provided valuable context on Core's involvement with similar initiatives across Australia (8:48-12:36). Core designed the Mackay Resource Centre of Excellence, which is scheduled for completion around December or January and is primarily focused on flotation capability with an emphasis on coal tailings processing. They are also reviewing the QR Cuff facility for Queensland government and have submitted proposals for a Tasmanian common user facility. David raised important concerns about market differentiation, questioning what distinguishes these government-sponsored facilities from existing commercial Met labs and whether there's sufficient demand to justify them (12:36-13:24).

**Red Metals as a Potential Anchor Tenant**
Dylan described Core's two-year involvement with Red Metals' Sybella Rare Earth Project, located 20 kilometres south of Mount Isa (13:33-15:33). The project involves large-scale heap leach test work, and they're now considering scaling up to demonstration-scale work. This aligns well with the Mount Isa Centre concept, as Red Metals has been discussing the need for larger scale demonstration work potentially conducted locally at Mount Isa rather than at Core's Brisbane facility (15:36-16:13). The timing and alignment of this need with the Centre's development could make Red Metals a key early user.

**Centre Differentiation and Capabilities**
Shane clarified the Centre's intended positioning in the market, emphasizing it will NOT compete with established labs like ALS or others on routine sampling and assays (16:49-19:54). Instead, the focus is on providing sovereign capability for complex work currently sent overseas, citing ultra-high purity quartz test work sent to Germany at three-quarters of a million dollars as an example. The Centre will offer scale capability including a hard stand area for demonstration plants that can be brought in temporarily, bulk beneficiation capability, and laboratory space adjacent to these larger-scale facilities. Shane noted that bulk beneficiation could tie in with Townsville's processing capabilities and would be particularly relevant for vanadium projects and tailings reprocessing from the region, with Julia Creek being 250 kilometres away.

**Site Selection and Layout**
The preliminary site planning allocates approximately 20 hectares on the northern fringe of Mount Isa, just north of the airport (20:13-22:35). The location offers good highway access, flat terrain, no sacred sites, and sufficient space for tailings and future expansion. Shane is conducting a site visit in approximately 10 days to evaluate this and other options. The facility design includes a large sample area for bulk work, lab-scale facilities, a small assays lab for quick turnaround (eliminating the weeks-long wait when samples are sent elsewhere), and additional lab space that visiting teams can use for project work in the region (22:35-24:44).

**Ultra High Purity Quartz Case Study**
David provided a highly relevant example of work Core is conducting with Ultra High Purity Quartz, where they've been replicating test work done at the renowned Dorfner facility in Germany and achieving comparable results (25:15-28:01). Stuart from Ultra High Purity Quartz is specifically seeking to build Australian capability and plans a pilot facility in Townsville, making him a strong candidate for Shane to engage. David cautioned that ultra-pure work (99.999% purity) requires dedicated spaces to prevent cross-contamination, which is an important design consideration for a multi-user facility.

**Funding and Operating Model**
The capital funding strategy targets grants and the Northern Australia Infrastructure Fund, with potential for significant US funding (28:01-29:34). The operating model remains open, with Shane favoring a tender process that results in a collaboration between a commercial operator, a university partner for applied research elements, and potentially an overseas partner focused on supply chain security. This hybrid model aims to balance commercial viability with research objectives.

**Critical Staffing and Cost Challenges**
David highlighted a significant challenge observed across all common user facilities: staffing continuity when work is intermittent (29:34-31:43). Skilled personnel cannot simply be let go during gaps and rehired later, yet maintaining staff without revenue creates financial pressure. Flying staff from other locations and providing accommodation escalates costs significantly. Shane addressed this by describing plans for on-site accommodation—single and two-bedroom apartments—that would facilitate short-term staff placement at reasonable cost. Additionally, a skills training centre on-site could provide an anchor activity for ongoing staffing, offering exploration, laboratory, and technical training for the regional workforce in a model similar to the School of Mines in Kalgoorlie.

**Next Steps and Technical Assistance**
Shane has budget allocation for a technical expert to assist with equipment specifications, staffing requirements, and detailed operating cost modeling (31:59-34:34). He offered to send Core the scope of work for their review and potential proposal. David expressed strong interest, noting Core's intimate knowledge of the space and their relationships with relevant projects could add value. Shane also noted this engagement would give Core insight into the facility should they consider bidding on an operating tender.

**Operating Risk and Commercial Viability**
David emphasized a critical lesson from other facilities: no operator will accept a tender where they're responsible for both business development and operations without demand certainty (34:34-36:35). Shane acknowledged this concern, confirming the understanding that gap funding will be necessary to provide runway for the Centre to establish itself. He stressed his focus on future-proofing the design—building it large enough from the start, as there's only one opportunity to get it right. Shane also candidly acknowledged that the feasibility study might ultimately conclude the project doesn't stack up commercially, and identified operating as "probably almost the most complex part" of the entire initiative (36:35).
