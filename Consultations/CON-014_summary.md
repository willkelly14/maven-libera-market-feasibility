# Nathan Chapman - Austral Copper Operations & Metallurgy - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-11-17

---

## Summary and Insights
**Project Context & Regional Focus**
The Mt Isa Research Centre feasibility study aims to establish a metallurgical testing lab to support the mining industry in northwest Queensland, with particular emphasis on rare earths but also broader base metals capabilities. The initiative is backed by Mt Isa City Council and Critical Minerals Queensland, responding to Glencore's impending closure and the growth of rare earth exploration in the region. (0:15 – 3:00)
**Austral Resources Operations**
Austral is copper-focused with diverse processing capability including sulphide flotation, gravity, and SXEW (solvent extraction–electrowinning). The company is acquiring the Loretta project, adding potential lead, zinc, and silver to its inventory. From a critical minerals perspective, cobalt represents the primary opportunity under Queensland's current definition. The company's 1.5 km heap leach facility (described as a "big sandcastle") is estimated to contain between 4,000 and 11,000 tonnes of cobalt that will be reprocessed, though grades are variable and not well characterized. (3:00 – 4:52)
**Metallurgical Testing & Lead Times**
Lead times for metallurgical testwork and geomechanical studies (drop weight, crushing, grinding, comminution) can extend to "quite a number of months" depending on lab volumes. Assay turnaround times are similarly affected by seasonal drilling volumes, particularly during the dry season when ASX-compliant turnaround becomes challenging. Having drop weight testing capability in Mt Isa would address a fundamental need—"the fundamentals are still absolutely required." (7:03 – 8:52)
**Research Support Infrastructure**
A small assay lab at the proposed centre would be valuable for supporting research activities. When facilities like JK Tech send small sample batches to commercial labs with thousands of samples in queue, research timelines blow out significantly. A dedicated research assay capability would eliminate this bottleneck. (9:19 – 11:00)
**Regional Oxide Variability & Heap Leach Opportunities**
Oxide copper variability across the Mount Isa region is extreme. In the western Lady Annie area, young cover preserves the oxidation profile to depths of up to 150 metres below surface, creating extensive copper carbonate zones amenable to heap leaching. In the eastern areas, oxide zones are much thinner—typically only 5 metres of genuine copper carbonate mineralisation suitable for heap leach. (11:00 – 13:05)
**Transitional Zone Challenge**
The transitional zone between oxide and sulphide represents both the greatest challenge and opportunity. This zone hosts extreme supergene-enriched copper grades alongside cobalt in sulphates, but mineralogy is complex—native copper, tenorite, cuprite, and chalcocite associations that "don't leach but contain an awful lot of copper and cobalt." Current recoveries in transitional material are typically 50% or lower, compared to 80–90% in pure oxides or concentrates. Unlocking value from this zone would be "immense" and technology "hasn't kind of caught up with the inventory." Any breakthrough would have application throughout Africa's similar copper deposits. (11:00 – 14:26)
**Acid Consumption & Cost Drivers**
Acid consumption is the base input cost for oxide copper operations. Austral sources sulphuric acid from Townsville at approximately $270 per tonne because Incitec Pivot/Dyno Nobel has locked up the supply contract from the Isa smelter. Development of phosphate mining in the region would provide access to cheaper local acid. Research into alternative chemistries—nitrate leaches, ammonia additions, or oxidizing agents like table salt or bleach—could improve copper and cobalt solubility while reducing acid consumption. (14:26 – 16:42)
**Demonstration Heap Leach Pad**
A demonstration-scale heap leach pad at the centre could be relatively modest—approximately 100m × 50m—with liner, drainage infrastructure, and sectioned layout to run multiple trials simultaneously with separate drainage collection and assay. Every new discovery in the Isa region will have an oxide component, making this infrastructure relevant across the exploration sector. (17:05 – 18:14)
**Pressure Oxidation as Strategic De-Risking**
Pressure oxidation (autoclave technology, originally the "gunpowder method") represents a pathway to de-risk the regional copper industry from dependence on Glencore's smelter. The process involves cooking chalcopyrite under pressure to auto-oxidize it to chalcocite, which is then amenable to leaching and production of LME A-grade copper cathode—a refined product requiring no further smelting or refining. This would provide sovereign capability and eliminate the "over a barrel" commercial position where Glencore pays scrap prices for premium cathode. With no large high-grade deposits ("330 million ton at 3%") on the horizon and future discoveries likely sub-1% grade, cost of production is king, and pressure oxidation capex is "substantially lower" than building or relining a smelter—essentially "an oxygen plant" added to existing float circuits. (18:24 – 25:35)
**Bulk Sample Testing & Sovereign Capability**
The centre's bulk sample beneficiation capability would enable local concentration of ores (e.g., rare earth projects) before shipping concentrates to Townsville, rather than transporting all ore and managing waste disposal off-site. This aligns with the broader sovereign capability objective. (21:42 – 22:23)
**Current Offshore Testing**
Austral has a half-tonne bulk composite metallurgical sample ready to ship to Chile (by sea freight) for confidential nitrate leach trials with international private enterprise. The company conducts digest, bottle roll, and sequential leach tests in-house to establish baseline recoveries, then engages external partners to optimize chemistry and kinetics. Columns are available at the existing SXEW plant but personnel constraints limit in-house R&D. (22:44 – 26:52)
**IP & Knowledge Sharing**
Commercial partners maintain confidentiality around proprietary leach formulations. There is an opportunity through a critical minerals initiative to make process optimizations (e.g., salt additions, reagent blends) publicly available where they would benefit the broader industry without harming individual operators. Resource constraints at operating companies mean "we just don't have the time to run the experiments," creating a clear role for a dedicated research centre. (26:52 – 27:38)
