# Rob Theron DevEx - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-11-17

---

## Summary and Insights

**DevEx Background and Projects**
Rob Theron joined DevEx approximately two months prior to this meeting (1:17), bringing broad mineral exploration experience across multiple commodities, though he is relatively new to uranium specifically (1:40). DevEx's portfolio includes flagship uranium projects in the Northern Territory and the Kennedy Rare Earth Element (REE) project in Queensland, an ionic clay deposit (4:10).

**Mt Isa Research Centre Project Context**
Shane provided background on the Centre's evolution: approximately 12 months ago, the Sustainable Minerals Institute (University of Queensland), Mt Isa City Council, and Critical Minerals Queensland commissioned a scoping study initially focused on rare earths as a de-risking strategy for Mt Isa in advance of potential smelter closure (1:49–2:52). The scope has since broadened significantly to encompass critical minerals across the Northwest Minerals Province extending into the Northern Territory and Northern Queensland, targeting complex testwork that currently requires sending samples interstate or overseas (2:52–3:35).

**Current Laboratory and Testing Practices**
DevEx conducts metallurgical testwork for the Kennedy Rare Earths project through **CSIRO and ANSTO** (the Australian Nuclear Science and Technology Organisation), keeping the work within Australia (5:07–5:39). For uranium analysis, DevEx uses major commercial laboratories including **Intertek and ALS**, which have facilities distributed across main centres including Perth and Townsville (5:45–6:59). Rob confirmed these labs are more than capable of handling the uranium analysis work (5:45–6:59).

**Bulk Testwork Requirements and Opportunities**
When asked about bulk testwork, Rob indicated DevEx's programs to date have involved "some hundreds of kgs of material" rather than tonnage-level quantities (8:23–8:44). He acknowledged that having local capacity for tonnage-level bulk testwork "would probably be very useful" (8:44–8:51). Shane explained the Centre would provide capability for controlled bulk testwork, which addresses the expense and time involved in transporting large sample volumes elsewhere (8:07–8:23).

**Bulk Sample Beneficiation Capability**
Shane outlined a key differentiator: the Centre will offer **bulk sample beneficiation** services. This would allow ore to be concentrated on-site before being sent to other facilities (such as common user facilities), rather than transporting all material and dealing with expensive waste disposal at the destination. The example given was vanadium beneficiation, with the concept applying wherever it makes economic sense to process material locally first (8:51–10:16).

**Learning Centre and Workforce Development**
The Centre will include a **learning centre** focused on upskilling the regional workforce, with training delivered locally rather than requiring people to travel elsewhere (10:16–10:30). Training scope may cover metallurgical processes, exploration sampling and testing, and professional development, potentially supported by a teaching laboratory (10:30–11:04). Rob noted this would provide valuable options for Mt Isa and help retain local population—a theme also discussed at the Society of Economic Geology conference in Brisbane where Mt Isa's viability post-smelter was addressed (10:42–11:34).

**Applied Research and Local Geology Focus**
Shane emphasized the Centre's evolution toward identifying specific capability gaps and providing **applied research into local geology and extraction methods**, particularly valuable for new entrants to the region (11:34–11:58).

**Operating and Funding Model**
The operating model has not been finalized, but the preferred approach combines **research and commercial activities** to attract and retain the necessary expertise, since trust in laboratory personnel, processes, and QA/QC is critical and difficult to build from scratch (11:58–12:56). Shane expressed increased confidence in funding compared to a year prior, noting the advantage of working in a sector that is currently "flavour of the month" (13:16–13:30).

Expected funding sources include the **Queensland government, federal government, and Northern Australia Infrastructure Fund**, with commercial revenue streams essential to avoid ongoing subsidy dependency (13:40–14:02). The commercial activities are designed to avoid competing with established laboratories' core business—focusing instead on bulk sampling, applied research on local geology, and the learning centre, rather than high-volume routine assay work (14:02–14:40).

**Technical Consultancy Reference**
Rob offered to share a recent article from the Australian Journal of Earth Sciences by consultancy **RSC**, examining characterisation of clay-hosted rare earth deposits. He suggested RSC as a potential point of contact, noting they are a well-established consultancy across the minerals industry with apparent focus on rare earths (14:40–15:48). Shane indicated interest in reviewing the paper and noted the project team is looking to appoint a technical person or organisation to complete the technical feasibility elements (15:15–16:13).

**R&D Tax Incentive Registration**
Shane raised the intention to register the Centre as an **R&D Centre** to enable clients to claim R&D tax rebates, noting that nearly all Centre activities would qualify as R&D with an approximately **40% rebate on spend**—a significant value proposition (17:16–17:27). Rob was uncertain whether DevEx currently claims R&D rebates, though Shane's research analyst would be reviewing this in DevEx's financial accounts (17:16–17:27).
