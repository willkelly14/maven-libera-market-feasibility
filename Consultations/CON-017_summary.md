# Paul Harris & Iain Miller - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-11-20

---

## Summary and Insights
**Research Centre Concept and Purpose**
The meeting focused on the Mt Isa Research Centre feasibility study being conducted by the Sustainable Minerals Institute (UQ) for Mount Isa City Council with support from Critical Minerals Queensland. The Centre was originally scoped for rare earths processing but has evolved to address broader metallurgical challenges (2:37-3:59). The primary goal is to build regional lab capability for complex metallurgical testwork that currently forces companies to send samples interstate or overseas, rather than competing with commercial assay labs like ALS on routine work (4:05-5:10). The Centre will also include a learning and skills training component to support workforce development in the region (3:59).

**Aeon Metals' Metallurgical Challenges and Testing History**
Aeon's Walford Creek deposit presents significant metallurgical complexity as a polymetallic ore body containing copper, cobalt, nickel, lead, zinc, and silver locked up in pyrite (iron sulfide) (5:22-7:35). While simple copper flotation is possible, accessing all metals requires sophisticated processing. The company's latest flowsheet involves pressure oxidation (POX) autoclave followed by electrowinning (6:35-7:35). Aeon has conducted testwork both domestically (Perth via ALS) and internationally, with bulk samples previously sent to Europe for the pre-feasibility study (5:22-7:35). Paul Harris emphasized that any facility in Mount Isa capable of supporting this type of complex testwork would be "brilliant, fantastic" (7:43-7:58).

**Strong Interest in Pressure Oxidation Capability**
Pressure oxidation emerged as a key capability requirement, with Shane confirming it "has come up several times" in stakeholder consultations (7:35-7:43). This validates the need for specialized autoclave testing capacity in the region. Paul recommended a future technical discussion with Dr. Fred Hess, Aeon's board director with a doctorate in metallurgy who leads their processing work, once facility specifications are being defined (16:20-16:42).

**Bulk Sampling and Integration with QR Cuff**
The Centre concept includes bulk sampling capability to enable beneficiation in the region, so only concentrates need to be transported to facilities like QR Cuff (Queensland Common User Facility) in Townsville for pilot-scale work (8:48-9:13). This approach would significantly reduce logistics costs and avoid expensive tailings disposal charges (approximately $1,000/tonne was mentioned) (8:48-9:13). Shane noted that one vanadium miner at Julia Creek is progressing rapidly and may pilot before QR Cuff is operational (0:21-1:09).

**Core Storage Facility -- A Newly Validated Need**
Core storage emerged as an important capability only raised this week in the feasibility process (18:06-18:42). Iain confirmed strong demand, noting that multiple companies regularly ask Aeon if they can store core at their facility in Mount Isa, but real estate costs make dedicated storage prohibitively expensive (18:42-20:19). The existing government core storage facility in Mount Isa is small and limited to cores donated to the department (18:42-20:19). Paul referenced the WB Clark Geoscience Centre in Londonderry, NSW, as a benchmark facility operating under the National Virtual Core Library program, and Iain described Adelaide's world-class climate-controlled facility in a refurbished auto assembly plant as exemplary (18:36-20:19). The concept of integrated core storage with meeting facilities would enable companies to host due diligence reviews when sites are inaccessible during wet season or non-drilling periods (25:31-25:58).

**Project Logistics and Regional Workforce Challenges**
Aeon's Walford Creek project is located approximately 350-400 km directly north of Mount Isa in the Gulf Country, with seasonal access constraints (9:21-10:20). While sample transportation via weekly or fortnightly freight is generally manageable, 2025 saw notable delays with ALS lab processing and freight logistics attributed to workforce uncertainty stemming from the Glencore smelter closure situation (10:53-12:21). This validates the Research Centre's workforce retention strategy through regional upskilling (10:20).

**Environmental Testing Capability**
In addition to metallurgy, the Centre will provide environmental testing services for water and soil (18:06).

**Multi-Purpose Learning and Meeting Facility**
The Centre will include a learning centre that can be hired by companies for investor roadshows, board meetings, and staff training programs -- addressing a gap in regional infrastructure for corporate and technical events (24:12-25:31). This multi-purpose approach allows core viewing, technical meetings, and stakeholder presentations in one location (25:31-26:01).

**Pilot Plant Hard Stand Concept**
Rather than building permanent pilot plant infrastructure, the facility will provide a hard stand area where companies can temporarily set up short-term pilot programs, access common-use beneficiation equipment, and utilize dedicated tailings and wastewater treatment infrastructure before demobilizing (26:01-26:59). This flexible model avoids creating stranded assets.

**Site Selection and Co-Location Opportunities**
The Council has identified land parcels north of the airport for evaluation, with plans to secure 20-50 hectares to avoid the land cost constraints of urban industrial estates (27:10-28:26). This scale enables future expansion and co-location of complementary facilities such as a quartz bagging facility and quartz core shack, creating a mining research precinct with highway access (27:10-28:26).

**Pyrite Roaster and Regional Sulfuric Acid Supply**
Paul raised a critical regional infrastructure issue: Mount Isa City Council's previous concept (led by Peter McRae's team) included a pyrite roaster as part of an industrial precinct alongside the Research Centre (28:26-31:13). This facility would address the looming sulfuric acid deficit once Glencore's smelter closes, as SO2 from the smelter currently supplies acid for copper oxide extraction, vanadium processing, and fertilizer production at Phosphate Hill (DynoNobel/Incitec Pivot, now up for sale and closing in six months) (29:37-31:01). Aeon has abundant pyrite material at Walford Creek but no economic incentive to build a dedicated roaster, as sulfuric acid production from pyrite (selling at $0-350/tonne) is only viable as a smelter by-product rather than a standalone operation (31:01-34:10). Paul emphasized this should be considered in conjunction with the Research Centre as it would solve long-term consumable supply issues for Northwest Queensland mineral production (31:01-34:43). Studies by Paul Holden's Critical Minerals Queensland group and the State Development Group have examined regional acid sourcing options (33:01-34:10).

**Government Liaison and Navigation Support**
Paul identified a major pain point for junior explorers: navigating Queensland's complex government structure with over 1,500 resources department employees across multiple agencies, each with sequential approval processes and funding buckets (21:38-23:43). Aeon employs Paul Fennelly (former Queensland Coordinator-General) specifically for this navigation role, and Paul suggested the Research Centre could host a dedicated explorer liaison officer within its administration building to provide roadmaps and guidance on approvals, funding pathways, and departmental sequencing (23:19-25:31). Iain reinforced the confusion around "which door opens next" and the sensitivity of departmental protocols (24:09). Shane noted Critical Minerals Queensland would be a logical fit for this function (25:31).

**Commercial Assay Lab Co-Location Model**
Shane proposed a hybrid model analogous to pathology services at GP clinics: building an assay lab sized for future commercial use, with a research-dedicated section, while allowing a third-party commercial operator to run routine assays on-site (12:21-13:31). This would restore commercial assay capacity to the region (currently all samples go to Brisbane, Adelaide, Townsville, or Perth despite work originating from Mount Isa) while supporting research work and enabling gradual transition to expanded research activity (14:31-16:08). Iain supported anything that benefits the broader region rather than a single entity, noting the high level of exploration activity across copper, lead, zinc, and uranium in the area (13:31-14:31).

**Funding Strategy**
The anticipated funding model combines Queensland government, federal government, and Northern Australia Infrastructure Fund support, reflecting the sovereign capability and regional minerals-based rationale for the investment (14:31-16:08). Shane noted the U.S. government is allocating $8.5 million to similar initiatives, and only a small portion would be needed for this facility (16:08).

**Key Stakeholder Endorsement**
Paul expressed strong support for Mount Isa Mayor Peter McRae (now referred to as "she" -- likely Cathy Martin or another official), describing her as "fantastic for the region," forward-thinking, down-to-earth, a proponent for mining, and effective in working with stakeholders to keep the smelter open (34:48-35:18). Shane also noted the progressive leadership of CEO Tim Rose (35:22).
