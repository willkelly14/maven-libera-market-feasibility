# MMG Consultation - Cathy Martin (Metallurgy) & Laura Moore (Geology) - Summary and Insights

**Date:** 2025-12-08

---

## Summary and Insights
### MMG's Current Lab Capabilities
**18:44-22:29** Cathy Martin provided a detailed overview of MMG's internal lab infrastructure. The facility operates with two crews of five people on rotating shifts and includes:
- Two XRF machines for main elements (zinc, lead, iron, manganese, and minor elements)
- An ultra machine for carbon and sulfur analysis
- Transitioning from AAS to ICP machine for silver and expanded mineral analysis
- UCS equipment for paste strength sampling for underground operations
- Standard lab equipment including sieves, lab float test cells, grinding mill, rug mill, and cyclosizer
- Site crew of approximately 400 people
**Key limitation:** The lab is not accredited, requiring shipment assays to be sent to external labs. Internal capability allows for rapid turnaround (2-3 hours for spot samples and onsite surveys), which is a significant advantage over their Rosebery operation where samples must be sent hours away.
### External Testing Requirements
**22:35-25:04** MMG sends various work externally:
- Sizing and mineralogy samples go to multiple providers
- Environment and geology samples go to ALS Brisbane
- Larger parcels of work and monthly composites requiring accreditation are sent away
- Water samples experience significant delays (two weeks turnaround time was noted as frustrating for other operators like CapCopper)
- Cathy has occasionally hand-delivered samples to Brisbane ALS lab to expedite turnaround times
**24:06-24:58** Water testing is performed by the Environmental team but not currently in the metallurgy lab. The new ICP machine may provide capacity for some water sampling. Routine water samples for process troubleshooting and quarterly monitoring all require external labs with lengthy turnaround times.
### Opportunities for Regional Testing Facility
**25:15-26:03** Shane introduced the concept of providing space for a commercial operator at the new facility to handle bulk assays and environmental testing. Cathy responded positively, noting this would significantly improve turnaround times and support local businesses. This model could address the current inefficiencies where companies are requesting samples be sent to Townsville because Mount Isa ALS collection point cannot keep up with demand.
**23:25-23:53** MMG has strong internal capabilities but relies on supplier-provided free test work (e.g., grinding media suppliers offer free test work for media wear rates and grind establishments for regrind media). A local facility would likely be preferred over external suppliers for such work.
### Exploration Testing Challenges
**32:18-34:41** Laura Moore highlighted significant exploration testing bottlenecks:
- ALS Mount Isa lab has requested samples be sent to Townsville due to capacity constraints
- MMG's exploration program includes work on molybdenum, boride, and boronite that often gets deprioritized
- Currently rely on sponsoring PhD students and university students to access specialized equipment for mineralogy and mineral testing
- Looking at garnets, aging, and other near-mine exploration targets that require university lab access through personal contacts
**34:06-34:41** The wet season creates a compressed timeframe for exploration work in North Queensland, making rapid sample turnaround even more critical compared to southern operations. Shane emphasized this as a key driver for building sovereign Research capability in the region.
### Acid Drainage Testing Opportunity
**34:41-36:24** Laura identified a specific high-volume testing need:
- Natural potential acid-forming rock testing (NAF and PAF) is becoming increasingly stringent for mine sites
- All samples currently must go offsite for testing
- Open pit operations down south can perform this in-house
- Underground mines in North Queensland face significant compliance requirements for waste dump storage
- This would generate substantial sample volume and consistent work
Shane noted this fits the model of providing space and equipment for a commercial operator at the Centre, combining environmental testing capabilities with Research lab activities.
### Geometallurgy Program
**37:32-38:41** Cathy mentioned MMG is initiating a geometallurgy program:
- Currently working with JK Tech Centre for hardness testing
- Both ad hoc and routine sampling programs
- Significant volume of samples being sent away that could potentially be done locally
**38:41** The discussion touched on JK Tech/SMI's Indooroopilly facility closure, as that site will struggle to conduct noxious Research when it transitions. Mount Isa's existing industrial character and infrastructure makes it well-suited to take on this type of work that cannot be performed in residential areas.
### Technology Development and Innovation
**18:42** Cathy noted MMG actively engages with UQ researchers on emerging technologies, citing a recent example of testing cantilever drag sensors as a mass flow proxy on float cells. This demonstrates openness to collaborative Research and willingness to trial new commercial technologies.
**15:50-16:50** Context from earlier discussion: Regional miners have specific Research needs, such as Harmony's interest in native copper processing Research to address concentrator issues, and numerous companies requiring flow sheet development for rare earths processing.
### Workforce and Training Opportunities
**38:51-40:11** Discussion of people and capacity building:
- Cathy raised the challenge of attracting researchers and technical staff to Mount Isa
- Council is considering accommodation facilities for visiting researchers, PhD students, and short-term workers
- **39:19-39:49** Shane outlined plans for a learning Centre with teaching lab for technician training in the region and to allow students to combine fieldwork with lab training
- **40:11** Cathy suggested the Centre could serve as a hub for graduate rotations, noting Fran Burgess has advocated for graduates to rotate through consulting companies for broader experience
**4:28-5:08** Context: MMG has a well-established graduate program with strong university engagement (particularly UQ), taking graduates across all departments including metallurgy, geology, environment, and engineering. The FIFO model from Brisbane is attractive for graduate recruitment.
### Timeline and Regional Context
**41:25-41:50** Feasibility study targeted for completion in early Q2 2026 (April-May timeframe), with best-case scenario of 1-2 years to get the facility operational.
**41:54-42:16** Laura noted the timing would align well with Harmony's operations ramping up. Recent positive news includes the smelter announcement and Harmony's development, which will boost regional economic activity and create additional demand for Research and testing services.
