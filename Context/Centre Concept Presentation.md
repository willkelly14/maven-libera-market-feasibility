> - **Centre-left (orange zones):** Sample preparation, comminution & characterisation; demonstration-scale comminution; demonstration-scale beneficiation — all fronted by an apron/awning area.
> - **Centre (green zones):** Metallurgical labs, assays lab, environmental lab, and staff amenity areas.
> - **Lower-centre:** Pilot plant area (with spare hardstand area), expansion area, bulk concentrates holding area, and future CM & REE storage facility.
> - **Bottom strip:** Bulk ore/tailings sample delivery & storage area (with truck loading bays), heap leach area, and machinery & equipment storage shed.

> - **Traffic flow arrows** show one-way truck access along the bottom and two-way service access.

### Bus Parking

Bus parking will cater for up to three large buses (or up to six smaller buses). Buses enter from the south, park, then exit from the east exit. This allows for one-way traffic flow, avoiding the need for buses to turn around (safer approach).

### Registering as an R&D Provider

The concept includes a registered premises for the R&D rebate scheme. This Federal Government Program provides companies with a refundable offset on eligible expenditure incurred on designated R&D projects. For companies with annual aggregated turnover of less than $20m, the offset rate is 43.5%. Explorers and smaller mining companies operating within the SOM spent approximately $44m on R&D activities for which they were able to claim an R&D offset. Metallurgical testing falls within the scope for the R&D offset program.

### Hard Rock Sample Area

- Catering for drill cores and bagged ore chips
- Scale is a key consideration — may need two separate areas:
  - Small-scale sampling for more complex metallurgy
  - Commercial assays lab operated by commercial partner
- Core storage area with racking system (similar to nearby drill core facility)
- P&E to include a forklift capable of extending to the full height of a 5-bay high rack

### Main Road Turn-Off

The concept allows for a single shared turn-off: light vehicles and buses enter the staff and visitor parking areas, while heavy vehicles use the two-way service lane for bulk ore, samples, consumables delivery, or bulk concentrates collection and waste removal.

### Bulk Sample Area

- Includes comminution and beneficiation equipment, initially focused on vanadium but with P&E for other critical minerals
- Demonstration plant scale to match the Townsville QRCUF facility
- 50 MT/day initial capacity with building design to allow doubling to 100 T/day
- The QRCUF facility has a concentrate capacity of 0.75 T/hr, or 7.5 T/day based on a 10-hour processing day (converts on a 1:5 feed ratio to ~37.5 T/day)
- Vanadium flowsheet will guide P&E selection

### Teaching Lab

Drawing on the WA Kalgoorlie School of Mines, the learning centre will feature a fully functional teaching lab providing a foundation for this to become a learning destination for university students, post-graduates, and established mining companies looking to upskill their in-house lab technicians.

### Bulk Concentrates

- Capacity roughly 20% of the delivery and storage area based on a 5:1 feed-to-concentrate ratio for vanadium
- Consider the need for pressure cleaning after batches have been processed
- May need to allow for covering (possibly tarping rather than a permanent structure)

## Slide 12 — Additional Design Notes (Continued)

> **[Diagram]** Same detailed facility floor plan as Slide 11, repeated for reference alongside the design notes on this slide.

### Apron with Awning

The concept provides for a 10m covered concrete apron, creating a safe and dry working space for smooth transition between the MCRC and the service road. During the dry season, the awning reduces heat; in the wet season, it provides protection from heavy rain with winds predominantly from the southeast.

### Vacuum Dust Extraction

Dust extraction in comminution areas is vital for worker safety and to minimise sample contamination. Areas requiring extraction include sample preparation areas, comminution areas, and the drop weight testing room. Extraction will include removing dust particles at room scale and direct extraction at the source (via extraction pipes attached to P&E that creates dust).

### Accommodation

The concept includes a 20-pax short-stay accommodation hub featuring six two-bedroom cabins and eight single cabins for visitors completing skills training, laboratory training, or professional development at the learning centre. The accommodation also allows for staff or contractors working for infrequent periods.

Most stays are expected to be 1–5 days but could extend up to one month for project-specific work (e.g., technicians in the commercial assays lab). The hub is not expected to cater for permanent employees, who will source rental accommodation in Mount Isa.

The accommodation hub would have its own booking system and is expected to be a profit centre. When not fully booked, external bookings would be accepted. Pricing is based on the Mapoon Aboriginal Shire Council's accommodation hub in Mapoon (one hour north of Weipa, Cape York).

**Features:**

- Cabins with kitchenettes
- Recreation area
- BBQ and covered outdoor eating area
- Laundry facilities
- On-site parking for 10 cars
- Wi-Fi
