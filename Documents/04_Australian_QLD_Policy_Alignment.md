# Australian & Queensland Policy Alignment Analysis

## Mount Isa Critical Minerals and Rare Earth Research Centre (MICMRC)

This document maps the proposed Critical Minerals Processing Centre concept against government policy at both federal and state levels, assessing how the Centre aligns with each policy objective, funding mechanism, and strategic priority. The analysis is structured to support government funding applications and form the policy alignment summary within the feasibility report.

---

## 1. Australian Critical Minerals Strategy 2023-2030

### 1.1 Overview

The Australian Critical Minerals Strategy 2023-2030 provides a framework for Australia to develop its critical minerals production, processing, and supply chains while creating jobs and economic opportunity. [04-001] The Strategy identifies 31 minerals as critical to the economy, national security, and the clean energy transition, including cobalt, lithium, manganese, rare earth elements, vanadium, graphite, tungsten, nickel, and high purity alumina — all of which are relevant to the Centre's target mineral suite. [04-005]

The Australian Government has stated its commitment to "building up our critical minerals capacity, fast-tracking the exploration, extraction, processing, and value-adding of these minerals." [04-006] The Centre directly supports this objective by providing processing research, testing, and demonstration-scale beneficiation capabilities in one of Australia's most mineral-rich regions.

### 1.2 Six Focus Areas and Centre Alignment

The Strategy is organised around six focus areas. [04-002] The Centre's alignment with each is assessed below.

**Focus Area 1: Developing Strategically Important Projects**

This focus area targets support to reduce project risks and attract commercial financing, backs exploration for new discoveries, reviews R&D support mechanisms, and aims to expand domestic refining and processing capabilities. [04-003] The Centre directly supports this priority by:

- Providing bench-scale mineral processing and metallurgical testing that de-risks projects before they proceed to bankable feasibility
- Operating as a registered R&D provider, enabling client companies to claim the 43.5% R&D tax offset on metallurgical testing conducted at the Centre
- Offering demonstration-scale comminution and beneficiation to prove process flowsheets at scale

**Alignment: Direct and strong.** The Centre is purpose-built to accelerate the development pathway for critical minerals projects.

**Focus Area 2: Attracting Investment and Building International Partnerships**

This focus area optimises trade and investment frameworks, facilitates business engagement, and seeks to attract foreign investment in project development and downstream processing. The Centre supports this objective by:

- Providing JORC-certified assay services that underpin the resource estimates foreign investors require
- Creating a visible, investable piece of critical minerals infrastructure in Queensland that demonstrates Australia's commitment to the sector

**Alignment: Indirect but supportive.** The Centre enhances Australia's credibility as a serious critical minerals partner.

**Focus Area 3: First Nations Engagement and Benefit Sharing**

This focus area strengthens partnerships respecting land and water rights, creates regional economic opportunities while protecting cultural heritage, and builds First Nations capacity to engage with industry. The Centre supports this objective through:

- Its location on Kalkadoon land, with the site plan explicitly referencing the mining precinct on Kalkadoon country
- Potential employment and training pathways for Indigenous community members through the Critical Minerals Learning Centre
- The accommodation hub model based on the Mapoon Aboriginal Shire Council's facilities

**Alignment: Direct, subject to genuine engagement.** The Centre's design already incorporates Indigenous-referenced elements and is sited to create local employment in a region with significant Indigenous population.

**Focus Area 4: Promoting ESG Leadership**

This focus area reinforces ESG credentials and shapes global standards for responsible energy transition. The Centre supports this by:

- Including an environmental testing laboratory (NATA certified) for water and soil testing
- Enabling responsible processing research that helps establish best-practice ESG standards for Australian critical minerals

**Alignment: Moderate.** The Centre's environmental lab directly supports ESG performance measurement.

**Focus Area 5: Unlocking Infrastructure Investment**

This focus area coordinates enabling infrastructure funding aligned with critical minerals projects. The Centre is complemented by major infrastructure investments including:

- CopperString 2032 ($5 billion) connecting the North West Minerals Province to the national grid [04-055]
- Queensland Government's $110 million supply chain improvement from Mount Isa to the Port of Townsville [04-060]
- $80 million Mount Isa Line Incentive Scheme to reduce rail access costs [04-060]

**Alignment: Strong synergy.** The Centre benefits from and reinforces the case for these infrastructure investments.

**Focus Area 6: Growing a Skilled Workforce**

This focus area expands education and specialist training availability and improves community understanding of mining's role in the energy transition. [04-004] The Centre directly delivers on this through:

- The Critical Minerals Learning Centre with a fully functional teaching lab
- Skills training and professional development programs modelled on the WA Kalgoorlie School of Mines
- Accommodation hub enabling residential training programs of 1-5 days or up to one month
- Upskilling pathways for in-house lab technicians from mining companies

**Alignment: Direct and strong.** The Centre is designed as a learning destination for universities, post-graduates, and industry professionals.

### 1.3 Future Made in Australia: Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentive

The Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentive (CMPTI), part of the Future Made in Australia package, provides a refundable tax offset of 10% of eligible processing costs for critical minerals processed in Australia. [04-007] The incentive is available from 1 July 2027 to 30 June 2040 for a maximum of 10 years per recipient. [04-007] The 2024-25 Federal Budget estimated the CMPTI at AU$7 billion over the decade. [04-009]

Eligible processing activities involve "substantially transforming a feedstock that contains a critical mineral into a purer or more refined form of the critical mineral that is chemically distinct from the feedstock." [04-008] Mining, beneficiation, and manufacturing do not qualify. [04-008]

**Centre alignment:** The Centre's hydrometallurgical laboratory, metal extraction lab, and pilot plant operations focus on exactly these kinds of transformation processes — leaching, refining, and extracting purified critical minerals from feedstock. While the Centre itself is primarily a research and testing facility, its work directly supports client companies seeking to establish CMPTI-eligible processing operations. The Centre can serve as the proving ground where processing flowsheets are developed and validated before commercial-scale facilities are built.

### 1.4 R&D Tax Incentive

The R&D Tax Incentive provides companies with annual aggregated turnover of less than $20 million a refundable tax offset of 43.5% on eligible expenditure incurred on designated R&D projects. [04-010] Metallurgical testing falls within the scope of the R&D offset program. [04-010] The Centre plans to register as an R&D provider, enabling its clients — particularly explorers and smaller mining companies — to claim this offset on testing conducted at the Centre.

**Centre alignment:** This is a core part of the Centre's value proposition. Explorers conducting metallurgical testing at a registered R&D facility can recover nearly half their costs through the tax system, making the Centre's services significantly more cost-effective.

## 2. Queensland Critical Minerals Strategy

### 2.1 Overview

The Queensland Critical Minerals Strategy represents a $245 million package of initiatives to grow Queensland's critical minerals sector. [04-050] Released in June 2023, the Strategy builds on the Queensland Resources Industry Development Plan and is specifically targeted at the critical minerals sector. [04-050]

Premier Palaszczuk stated Queensland's ambition is for the state to be "a global leader, supplier and manufacturer of critical minerals" rather than remaining a "low-end supplier," by processing materials and manufacturing renewables domestically. [04-054] This vision is precisely what the Centre is designed to enable.

### 2.2 Four Key Objectives and Centre Alignment

**Objective 1: Move Faster, Smarter**

Accelerate critical minerals development by improving government processes, maximising existing and abandoned mine potential, fostering partnerships, and investing in infrastructure and exploration. The Centre supports this by:

- Providing local testing and processing research that eliminates the need to send samples interstate or overseas, accelerating project timelines
- Enabling rapid metallurgical assessment of abandoned mine waste and tailings for critical minerals recovery potential
- Located in a designated Critical Minerals Zone (Mount Isa) for maximum strategic impact

**Alignment: Direct and strong.**

**Objective 2: Maximise Investment**

Establish favourable conditions to attract long-term investments in mining, processing, and advanced manufacturing. The Centre supports this by:

- Providing the testing infrastructure that underpins investment-grade resource assessments
- Demonstrating Queensland's commitment to building a complete critical minerals ecosystem
- Creating a facility that international investors can visit to see Queensland's capabilities firsthand

**Alignment: Strong.**

**Objective 3: Build Value Chains**

Develop onshore processing and manufacturing capabilities beyond extraction. This is the Centre's core mission — providing the R&D and testing infrastructure needed to develop processing flowsheets for critical minerals found in Queensland, enabling the transition from raw ore export to refined product export.

**Alignment: Direct and fundamental.** The Centre exists to enable this objective.

**Objective 4: Foster Research and ESG Excellence**

Partner with industry and research organisations to improve ESG performance while reducing costs. The Centre delivers on this by:

- Operating as a dedicated research facility partnered with universities and industry
- Including an environmental testing laboratory for ongoing ESG monitoring
- Providing a platform for collaborative research with CSIRO, Queensland universities, and industry bodies

**Alignment: Direct and strong.**

### 2.3 Critical Minerals Zones

The Queensland Government has established Critical Mineral Zones, with the first zones located at Mount Isa and Julia Creek/Richmond, supported by $75 million in funding for advanced critical minerals projects. [04-052] The zones adopt a place-based approach, enabling collaboration, common-user infrastructure, and baseline environmental and social impact assessments.

**Centre alignment:** The Centre is located within the Mount Isa Critical Minerals Zone. It represents the type of common-user infrastructure the Zones policy is designed to support — a shared facility that serves multiple projects rather than a single mine. The Centre is a direct embodiment of the Zones concept.

### 2.4 Key State Funding Mechanisms

**Queensland Critical Minerals and Battery Technology Fund ($100 million)**

The Fund, managed by QIC and Queensland Treasury, provides two assistance streams: government grants of up to $2 million (50% of project costs) for feasibility studies, pilot projects, and early operational activities; and equity/debt investment of up to $30 million for growth-stage businesses. [04-057] Eligible projects include establishing enabling infrastructure for local supply chain growth, building Queensland's capabilities in critical minerals extraction and processing, and creating ongoing Queensland-based jobs.

Treasurer Dick described the fund's primary goal as positioning Queensland "at the front of the queue with capacity and capability to meet global demand." [04-057]

**Centre alignment:** The Centre could access both streams — the grant stream for feasibility and pilot work, and potentially the investment stream for construction and operations. The Centre directly addresses three of the four eligible project types: enabling infrastructure, building extraction/processing capabilities, and creating Queensland jobs.

**Exploration Permit Rent Reduction**

From 1 September 2023, the Queensland Government reduced exploration permit rent to $0 for five years, representing approximately $55 million in forgone revenue. [04-053] This incentive increases exploration activity across Queensland, which in turn increases demand for the Centre's sample testing and assay services.

**Circular Economy R&D ($8 million) and Collaborative Exploration ($5 million)**

These additional allocations support research into mine waste reprocessing and collaborative exploration — both activities the Centre is designed to facilitate through its metallurgical testing and sample preparation capabilities. [04-058]

### 2.5 CopperString 2032

The Queensland Government is investing $5 billion in CopperString 2032, a high-voltage transmission line connecting the North West Minerals Province to the national electricity grid. [04-055] This infrastructure investment is transformative for the Centre because it delivers reliable, affordable, and renewable power to Mount Isa — a critical requirement for energy-intensive mineral processing research and operations.

### 2.6 North West Minerals Province Strategic Blueprint

The North West Minerals Province is assessed to hold mineral deposits worth $500 billion, making it one of the world's richest mineral-producing regions. [04-056] The Queensland Government's Strategic Blueprint for the Province included $110 million for supply chain improvements, including the $80 million Mount Isa Line Incentive Scheme for rail and $30 million for the Port of Townsville. [04-060] These investments directly support the movement of bulk samples to the Centre and processed concentrates from the Centre to export markets.

## 3. Critical Minerals Facility (Export Finance Australia)

### 3.1 Program Details

Export Finance Australia manages the Australian Government's $5 billion Critical Minerals Facility, the single largest federal funding mechanism for critical minerals projects. [04-100] The Facility provides financial expertise and solutions "to support Australian critical minerals projects and related infrastructure to help Australia become a leading provider of critical minerals globally." [04-100]

Financing takes the form of loans, loan guarantees, bonds, and working capital support. [04-101] Projects must:

- Demonstrate Australian benefit through extraction and/or processing of minerals in Australia for export
- Involve minerals identified in the Critical Minerals Strategy
- Have a completed comprehensive feasibility study
- Show buyer commitment to purchase production
- Use proven processing technology
- Demonstrate financial, technical, and commercial capacity

### 3.2 Centre Alignment

The Centre aligns with the Critical Minerals Facility in two ways:

1. **As potential direct applicant:** The Centre itself, once developed to include commercial-scale processing activities, could seek CMF financing for infrastructure expansion. However, the CMF primarily targets production projects rather than research facilities, so this pathway is secondary.

2. **As an enabler for CMF applicants:** More significantly, the Centre helps its client projects meet CMF eligibility criteria. By providing metallurgical testing, feasibility-grade process flowsheets, and demonstration-scale proof of concept, the Centre generates the technical evidence that projects need to satisfy CMF due diligence. A project that has validated its processing technology at the Centre is better positioned to demonstrate "proven processing technology" and meet "comprehensive feasibility study" requirements.

## 4. Northern Australia Infrastructure Facility (NAIF)

### 4.1 Program Details

NAIF provides financial assistance to projects and businesses in northern Australia, defined as areas of Queensland and Western Australia above the Tropic of Capricorn and the entire Northern Territory. [04-103] NAIF primarily provides loans above $10 million and equity investments — it does not provide grants or fund business cases. [04-103, 04-156]

As at 2024, NAIF has committed $4.3 billion across 32 projects, with $33 billion in forecasted public benefit and over 18,100 projected jobs. [04-106] As part of the Critical Minerals Strategy, NAIF has earmarked $500 million specifically for critical minerals project development. [04-105]

### 4.2 NAIF Investment Mandate Criteria and Centre Alignment

NAIF's investment mandate has four criteria, each of which the Centre satisfies: [04-104]

| NAIF Criterion | Centre Alignment |
|---|---|
| **Generate public benefit beyond the project** | The Centre serves multiple mining projects and explorers, not just one operation. Its learning centre, workforce training, and environmental testing provide broad community benefit. |
| **Stimulate longer-term economic and population growth** | The Centre creates permanent skilled employment in Mount Isa, attracts visiting researchers and industry professionals, and helps diversify the regional economy beyond single-mine dependency. |
| **Encourage private-sector participation** | The Centre is designed as a commercially operated facility with private sector clients. Its accommodation hub and commercial assay lab generate revenue from private industry. |
| **Promote sustainable Indigenous participation** | The Centre is located on Kalkadoon land with opportunities for Indigenous employment and training through the Learning Centre. The accommodation model references Indigenous community facilities. |

### 4.3 Geographic Eligibility

Mount Isa is located at approximately 20.7 degrees South latitude, well north of the Tropic of Capricorn (23.4 degrees South). The Centre is therefore within NAIF's eligible geographic area. Combined with the $500 million critical minerals earmark, NAIF represents a strong potential financing pathway for the Centre's infrastructure development.

## 5. CMCI CRC (Critical Metals for Critical Industries)

### 5.1 Program Details

The Critical Metals for Critical Industries (CMCI) CRC has been awarded $53 million in federal funding under the Cooperative Research Centres program. [04-150] The CRC brings together 62 partners, including 43 Australian businesses, 11 research organisations, and 4 peak bodies, who contribute a further $185 million, bringing the total program value to approximately $240 million. [04-150]

The CMCI CRC is designed to develop "next-generation onshore refinery capabilities for the high-demand ores and rare earths needed to manufacture renewable energy and digital technologies." [04-153] Its research programs span five themes: [04-151]

1. Commercial Pathways for CM Production
2. Technology-to-Market Acceleration
3. Professional Services Ecosystem
4. Value Chain Integration
5. HDR Training, Workforce and Capability Development

Partners include Queensland-based institutions QUT and the University of Queensland, alongside Mining3, Rozetta Institute, the Western Australian state government, Curtin University, and Swinburne University. [04-152]

### 5.2 Centre Alignment

The Centre aligns with all five CMCI CRC research themes:

| CRC Theme | Centre Capability |
|---|---|
| **Commercial Pathways for CM Production** | The Centre provides bench-scale and demonstration-scale processing research that develops commercial processing pathways |
| **Technology-to-Market Acceleration** | The pilot plant and heap leach trial areas allow new processing technologies to be tested at meaningful scale before commercial deployment |
| **Professional Services Ecosystem** | The commercial assay lab, environmental testing lab, and metallurgical services create a professional services cluster in Mount Isa |
| **Value Chain Integration** | The Centre connects exploration (sample testing) through processing (beneficiation, hydromet) to product (concentrates storage), integrating the value chain |
| **HDR Training, Workforce and Capability Development** | The Learning Centre with teaching lab provides exactly the workforce development infrastructure this CRC theme targets |

The Centre could participate in the CMCI CRC as a facility partner, providing physical infrastructure for CRC research programs. With QUT and the University of Queensland already CRC partners, there is a natural pathway for Queensland-based collaboration.

### 6.4 Conclusion

The proposed Centre demonstrates strong alignment with government policy at both federal and state levels. Every major policy objective in both the Australian Critical Minerals Strategy 2023-2030 and the Queensland Critical Minerals Strategy is either directly served or materially supported by the Centre's capabilities.

The Centre's strongest alignments are with:

1. **Value-adding and downstream processing** — both federal and state strategies prioritise moving beyond raw mineral export, which is the Centre's core purpose
2. **Workforce development** — the Learning Centre directly delivers on Federal Focus Area 6 and Queensland Objective 4
3. **Critical Minerals Zone designation** — the Centre is located within one of Queensland's first two designated Critical Minerals Zones
4. **NAIF eligibility** — Mount Isa's geographic position, the Centre's public benefit profile, Indigenous engagement potential, and private sector participation all satisfy NAIF's investment mandate
5. **R&D infrastructure** — the Centre's design as a registered R&D provider enables the tax system to subsidise its clients' testing costs

The total quantum of government support available to the critical minerals sector exceeds $14 billion across federal and state programs, demonstrating sustained bipartisan commitment to the sector. [04-154] The Centre is positioned at the intersection of these funding streams, with multiple potential pathways for both direct funding and indirect support through enhanced client project viability.
