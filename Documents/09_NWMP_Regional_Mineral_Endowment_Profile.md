# NWMP Regional Mineral Endowment Profile

## 1. Introduction

The North West Minerals Province (NWMP) covers approximately 375,000 square kilometres of North West Queensland, encompassing the towns of Mount Isa, Cloncurry, and Julia Creek [01-293]. It is one of the world's richest mineral-producing regions, with a known in-ground resource value estimated at more than $680 billion across multiple commodities, including new economy minerals valued at approximately $500 billion [01-294]. Seventy percent of Queensland's base metals and minerals are found within the NWMP, spanning copper, zinc, lead, silver, gold, phosphate, vanadium, cobalt, rare earth elements, molybdenum, and rhenium [01-295].

This profile compiles JORC-compliant resource estimates, current production data, national resource context, the project development pipeline, and the mineralogical characteristics that define the region — establishing the feedstock base that underpins the business case for the Mount Isa Critical Minerals and Rare Earth Research Centre (MICMRC).

## 2. Resource Estimates by Commodity

### 2.1 Zinc-Lead-Silver

The NWMP hosts some of the world's largest zinc-lead-silver deposits. Glencore's George Fisher operation (incorporating the former Hilton mine) held total reserves of 79 Mt at 7.4% zinc, 3.6% lead, and 66 g/t silver as at December 2019, comprising George Fisher North (56 Mt proven and probable), George Fisher South (17.5 Mt), and Lady Loretta (5.5 Mt at 13.4% zinc, 4.9% lead, and 83 g/t silver) [01-296].

MMG's Dugald River deposit, 65 km northwest of Cloncurry, holds a JORC mineral resource of 55 Mt at 13.4% zinc, 2.1% lead, and 36 g/t silver — one of the highest-grade zinc deposits globally [01-298].

South32's Cannington mine holds an ore reserve of 10 Mt with a 53 Mt underground mineral resource and a potential open-pit development that could unlock an additional 25 Mt [01-328].

### 2.2 Copper-Gold

The Ernest Henry copper-gold mine holds a JORC Mineral Resource of 97.1 Mt at 1.30% copper and 0.76 g/t gold (as at 31 December 2023), with a mine life extended to 2040 [01-297].

AIC Mines' Eloise Project holds total Mineral Resources of 22.9 Mt at 2.1% Cu and 0.5 g/t Au for 471,950 t Cu and 353,950 oz Au contained [01-314]. The adjacent Jericho deposit, with development commenced in June 2024, will lift combined production to more than 20,000 t/y of copper [01-314].

Chinova Resources operates the Osborne copper-gold processing facility 190 km south-east of Mount Isa, with total resources of 3.9 Mt of copper, 6.8 Moz of gold, 96,000 t of molybdenum, and 12,000 t of cobalt across multiple IOCG deposits including the Mount Elliott-SWAN system [01-312].

The Capricorn Copper mine, approximately 120 km north of Mount Isa, is a high-grade copper and silver underground mine operated by 29Metals with five JORC 2012 compliant deposits [01-313].

### 2.3 Vanadium

The NWMP hosts three world-class vanadium projects in the Toolebuc Formation oil shale between Julia Creek and Richmond:

- **Richmond Vanadium Project**: JORC 2012 Mineral Resource of 1.8 Bt at 0.36% V2O5 for 6.7 Mt of contained vanadium pentoxide — the largest non-titanomagnetite vanadium deposit of its kind globally — with a maiden ore reserve of 459.2 Mt at 0.49% V2O5 for 2.25 Mt of contained V2O5 [01-299].
- **Julia Creek Vanadium and Energy Project**: JORC Resource of 2,867 Mt at 0.31% V2O5 (461 Mt Indicated, 2,406 Mt Inferred), one of the single largest vanadium deposits in the world [01-300].
- **Saint Elmo Vanadium Project**: JORC resource of 546 Mt at 0.29% V2O5 for 1.58 Mt of contained vanadium pentoxide [01-301].

Australia holds the world's number one ranking for vanadium Economic Demonstrated Resources (10,173 kt EDR) with total ore reserves of 1,683 kt, yet has no current vanadium production [01-320]. The NWMP hosts the vast majority of these resources.

### 2.4 Cobalt

The Walford Creek copper-cobalt project, 350 km north-west of Mount Isa, is arguably the largest primary cobalt deposit in Australia [01-302]. Drilling in 2024 returned 48 m at 1.78% Cu and 0.33% Co — the highest copper-cobalt intercept ever recorded at the deposit [01-302]. Cobalt is also commonly found as a by-product of copper mining throughout the NWMP, with Mount Isa copper orebodies historically containing associated cobalt.

Australia's total cobalt EDR stands at 1,697 kt with reserves of 644 kt and annual production of 4.8 kt, giving a reserve life of 134 years [01-321].

### 2.5 Rare Earth Elements

Rare earth elements occur throughout the NWMP in several geological associations:

- **Korella rare earth deposit**: 150 km south-east of Mount Isa, with a JORC inferred resource of 7.03 Mt at 0.96 kg/t yttrium oxide [01-303]. The yttrium is contained in xenotime, a mineral amenable to hydro-metallurgical extraction [01-303].
- **Mary Kathleen**: The historical uranium-REE deposit, 60 km east of Mount Isa, originally contained about 200,000 t of rare earth oxides, with its tailings classified as one of the world's 25 highest-grade REE deposits [01-334].
- **Phosphorite-hosted REE**: Total identified phosphorite resources in the region exceed 1,500 Mt, with significant REE enrichment associated with Queensland's giant phosphorite deposits [01-335].

### 2.6 Phosphate

The NWMP's phosphate resources are globally significant. The Paradise South deposit holds a total JORC resource of 825 Mt with a Proven Ore target above 118 Mt (JORC 2012 Measured), supporting a mining plan of up to 2 Mt of phosphate ore per annum over an initial 20-year mine life [01-304].

Incitec Pivot's Phosphate Hill operation, 140 km south-east of Mount Isa, has an annual DAP/MAP production capacity of 975,000 tonnes, producing 739,500 t in FY2024 [01-305].

### 3.2 Glencore's Mount Isa Transition

After 60 years of copper mining, Glencore's Mount Isa underground copper mines (Enterprise, X41, and Black Rock) and copper concentrator closed in the second half of 2025, with the remaining mineral resources no longer economically viable due to low ore grades and ageing infrastructure [01-309]. However, the George Fisher zinc mine continues operating with a mine life extended to 2042 [01-310].

The Mount Isa copper smelter — which provides half of Australia's copper smelting capacity and treats more than one million tonnes of concentrate per year from miners throughout Australia — secured a Heads of Agreement for up to A$600 million in combined Australian and Queensland Government support to continue operating as a third-party concentrate processor until the end of 2028 [01-311]. This transition transforms the smelter from a captive facility into a regional processing hub for copper concentrates from across the NWMP and beyond.

Glencore has invested approximately A$10 billion in the Mount Isa region since 2003, with operating expenditure of approximately A$30 billion, contributing A$19.6 billion to regional, state, and national economies in 2024 [01-315].

### 3.3 Ernest Henry Operations

Ernest Henry, operated by Evolution Mining (100% ownership since January 2022), is one of Australia's major copper-gold producers [01-306]. Located 38 km northeast of Cloncurry, the sub-level caving underground operation mines 6.3 Mt of ore per annum via a 1,000 m hoisting shaft [01-306]. FY2025 production was 70,625 t copper and 47,776 oz gold, with FY2026 guidance of 65,000-70,000 t copper and 45,000-51,000 oz gold [01-306].

### 3.4 Dugald River Operations

MMG's Dugald River mine achieved zinc production of 163,588 t in 2024, an 8% lift from the prior year, with 2025 guidance of 170,000-185,000 t [01-307]. The mine employs approximately 500 workers and has an estimated mine life of 20+ years, processing 1.7 Mt of ore per annum [01-307].

## 4. NWMP Share of National Resources

### 4.1 Queensland's Dominant Position

Queensland holds approximately 60% of Australia's Economic Demonstrated Resources for zinc, lead, and silver, with the majority concentrated in the NWMP [01-316]. Queensland produces more than 20% of Australia's total copper output, with copper exports valued at A$3 billion in 2024 (22% of Queensland's minerals exports) [01-317].

### 4.2 Australia's Global Rankings

Australia maintains the world's number one ranking for EDR of lead, vanadium, and zinc, and ranks in the global top five for copper, cobalt, and silver (AIMR 2025) [01-318]. The table below summarises Australia's total resource position for NWMP-relevant commodities as at 31 December 2024 [01-319]:

**Vanadium Projects:**

- **PhosOne Project** (North West Phosphate/Sibanye-Stillwater JV): Detailed design, 490 construction and 240 operational jobs [01-326]

**Critical Minerals:**
- **Vecco Critical Minerals Project**: $798.1M, bankable feasibility by 2025, 300 construction and 274 operational jobs [01-325]

### 5.2 Enabling Infrastructure

The CopperString 2032 project — 840 km of new electricity transmission from south of Townsville to Mount Isa at a cost of $13.9 billion — will connect the NWMP to the National Electricity Market [01-323]. Major construction is targeted from 2028, with commercial operations by 2032 [01-323]. This infrastructure will reduce energy costs for existing and planned mining operations, remove a key barrier to project viability for the vanadium and phosphate projects, and support the transition to renewable energy for the region.

### 5.3 Exploration Activity

Queensland's mineral exploration expenditure reached $919.7 million in the 12 months to June 2024, an increase of $148.3 million (19%) from the prior year [01-329]. The NWMP is the highest-value mineral province in the state, attracting significant exploration investment from major international companies [01-329].

## 6. Mineralogical Characteristics

### 6.1 Deposit Diversity in the Mount Isa Inlier

The Mount Isa Inlier is a world-class base-metal province hosting a remarkable diversity of mineral deposit types [01-330]:

- **Sediment-hosted Cu-Zn-Pb deposits** (Mount Isa): Stratiform orebodies in dolomitic and carbonaceous pyritic shales [01-333]
- **Iron Oxide-Copper-Gold (IOCG) deposits** (Ernest Henry, Starra, Osborne): Hydrothermal Cu-Au systems with abundant iron oxides [01-331]
- **REE-Uranium deposits** (Mary Kathleen): Skarn-hosted with allanite and stillwellite [01-339]
- **Gold deposits** (Tick Hill): Unique high-grade gold-only mineralisation [01-330]
- **Mo-Re deposits** (Merlin): One of the world's highest-grade molybdenum-rhenium deposits [01-330]
- **Sediment-hosted copper** (Capricorn Copper): Breccia-hosted and shear-hosted mineralisation [01-338]

### 6.2 Polymetallic IOCG Deposits

IOCG deposits are a defining feature of the Eastern Fold Belt of the Mount Isa Inlier. These deposits exhibit distinctive enrichments of elements in the chalcophile-siderophile suite (Co, Ni, Bi, Se, Te) and the LILE-HFSE suite (REE, U, F, Ba, Mo), making them inherently polymetallic [01-332]. The Starra deposits in the Cloncurry district are hosted in north-trending zones of deformed iron-rich metasedimentary rocks [01-331].

Chinova Resources' Osborne complex exemplifies this polymetallic character, with its resource base spanning copper, gold, molybdenum, cobalt, and rhenium across multiple deposit styles including the Mount Elliott-SWAN IOCG system, the Kulthor carbonate vein-hosted deposit, the Mount Dore copper-oxide deposit, and the Merlin Mo-Re deposit [01-337].

The polymetallic nature of these ores creates significant processing complexity, requiring specialised metallurgical testing for flowsheet development — a core capability planned for MICMRC.

### 6.3 Stratiform Zinc-Lead-Silver Deposits

The George Fisher deposit comprises 11 stratiform orebodies striking near north-south and dipping west between 30-90 degrees, with mineralisation in galena and sphalerite-rich bedding-parallel horizons in dolomitic and variably carbonaceous pyritic shales [01-333]. These complex, fine-grained polymetallic ores require sophisticated beneficiation testing to optimise recovery.

### 6.4 Vanadium-Bearing Oil Shales

The NWMP's vanadium deposits occur in the Toolebuc Formation, a shallow sedimentary unit between Julia Creek and Richmond [01-336]. The formation is uniform in nature and amenable to standard open-cut mining [01-336]. Fresh oil shale below 15 m vertical depth contains in excess of three billion barrels of oil across a 600 km2 area, with vanadium co-occurring with kerogen-rich material [01-336]. The dual vanadium-energy value proposition and the need for novel processing flowsheets (beneficiation, leaching, and refining) represent a significant opportunity for applied research.

### 6.5 Rare Earth Mineralogy

The NWMP's rare earth mineralogy is complex and distributed across multiple geological settings:

- **Xenotime-hosted yttrium** at Korella, amenable to hydro-metallurgical extraction [01-303]
- **Allanite and stillwellite** at Mary Kathleen, associated with garnet-diopside skarn in the Mary Kathleen Domain [01-339]
- **Phosphorite-associated REE** throughout the region, with total identified phosphorite resources exceeding 1,500 Mt [01-335]
- **IOCG-associated REE enrichment** in the Cloncurry district [01-335]

This mineralogical diversity — spanning heavy and light rare earths, conventional and unconventional host minerals, and primary and secondary (tailings) sources — underscores the need for specialised characterisation and extraction research capabilities in the region.

### 6.6 Sediment-Hosted Copper Deposits

The Capricorn Copper deposits exhibit two dominant mineralisation styles: breccia-hosted mineralisation within quartzites (Mammoth and Greenstone orebodies in Whitworth Quartzite) and shear-hosted mineralisation within shales and siltstones (Esperanza, Pluto, and Esperanza South orebodies in McNamara Group siltstones) [01-338]. Each style requires different processing approaches, further illustrating the metallurgical complexity of NWMP ores.

## 7. Implications for MICMRC

The mineral endowment profile of the NWMP establishes several compelling drivers for the research centre:

1. **Feedstock diversity**: The region's eleven-plus commodity types and multiple deposit styles generate a continuous pipeline of metallurgical testing requirements — from routine assays to novel flowsheet development.

2. **Processing complexity**: Polymetallic IOCG ores, fine-grained stratiform zinc-lead deposits, vanadium-bearing oil shales, and complex rare earth mineralogy all require specialised characterisation and beneficiation research that cannot be adequately served by distant facilities [01-332] [01-333] [01-336].

3. **Scale of activity**: With billions of dollars in committed project investment, expanding exploration expenditure [01-329], and major operational transitions (Glencore's smelter becoming a third-party processor) [01-311], demand for local testing and research services is growing.

4. **Emerging commodities**: The NWMP's untapped vanadium resources (world's largest) [01-320], growing cobalt endowment [01-302], and rare earth potential [01-335] align directly with global demand for energy transition minerals, creating a pipeline of projects that will require processing R&D from feasibility through to production.

5. **Regional gap**: No equivalent research and testing facility exists within the NWMP, requiring samples to be transported thousands of kilometres to coastal laboratories — adding cost, delay, and logistical complexity for operators and explorers.
