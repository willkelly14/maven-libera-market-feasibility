## 1. AUSTRALIAN GOVERNMENT POLICY & STRATEGY

### 1.1 Australian Critical Minerals Strategy 2023–2030
- **Local file:** `01_Macro_Context/AUS Critical Minerals Strategy.pdf` (16 MB)
- **Publisher:** Commonwealth Department of Industry, Science and Resources (June 2023)
- **Web:** https://www.industry.gov.au/publications/critical-minerals-strategy-2023-2030
- **Key content:** 6 strategic objectives — develop strategically important projects, attract investment, First Nations engagement, ESG performance, enabling infrastructure, skilled workforce
- **Critical minerals list:** 31 commodities (nickel added Feb 2024)
- **List reference:** https://www.industry.gov.au/publications/australias-critical-minerals-list-and-strategic-materials-list

### 1.2 Future Made in Australia / Production Tax Incentive
- **Web:** https://www.industry.gov.au/mining-oil-and-gas/minerals/critical-minerals
- **Key data points:**
  - A$566.1 million over 10 years from 2024–25 for data, maps and tools for the resources industry
  - Critical Minerals Production Tax Incentive: 10% of processing/refining costs, up to 10 years per project (production between 2027–28 and 2039–40)
- **Parliamentary brief:** https://www.aph.gov.au/About_Parliament/Parliamentary_departments/Parliamentary_Library/Research/Policy_Briefs/2025-26/Criticalminerals

### 1.3 Critical Minerals Strategic Reserve (April 2025)
- **Web:** https://www.pmc.gov.au/domestic-policy/critical-minerals-strategic-reserve
- **Key data point:** $1.2 billion allocated in 2025–26 federal budget

### 1.4 Austrade — Critical Minerals Investment
- **Web:** https://international.austrade.gov.au/en/do-business-with-australia/sectors/energy-and-resources/critical-minerals

## 5. GLOBAL DEMAND & COMMODITY OUTLOOK

### 5.1 IEA — Global Critical Minerals Outlook 2025
- **Web:** https://www.iea.org/reports/global-critical-minerals-outlook-2025
- **Executive summary:** https://www.iea.org/reports/global-critical-minerals-outlook-2025/executive-summary
- **Full PDF:** https://iea.blob.core.windows.net/assets/ef5e9b70-3374-4caa-ba9d-19c72253bfc4/GlobalCriticalMineralsOutlook2025.pdf
- **Key data points:**
  - Lithium demand: 5× growth by 2040; graphite + nickel: 2× by 2040
  - Cobalt + rare earths: ↑50–60% by 2040; copper: ↑30% by 2040
  - Copper mine pipeline: potential 30% supply shortfall by 2035
  - Investment required: USD $500–600 billion in new mining capex to 2040
  - Top-3 mining country market share: 73% (2020) → 77% (2024)

### 5.2 IEA — The Role of Critical Minerals in Clean Energy Transitions
- **Web:** https://www.iea.org/reports/the-role-of-critical-minerals-in-clean-energy-transitions
- **Key data points:**
  - Mineral demand for clean energy nearly triples by 2030, quadruples by 2040 (NZE Scenario)
  - EV battery demand: ~1 TWh (2024) → 3+ TWh (2030)
  - Existing mines + projects under construction meet only ~50% of lithium/cobalt needs and ~80% of copper needs by 2030

### 5.3 IEA — Global EV Outlook 2025
- **Web:** https://www.iea.org/reports/global-ev-outlook-2025
- **Key data points:**
  - 17.1 million EVs sold in 2024
  - Projected: 40–50 million EVs by 2030 (STEPS/APS); 70 million (NZE)

### 5.4 World Bank — Commodity Markets Outlook
- **October 2025 report:** https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/entities/publication/d08d3ee8-e38a-4f02-8ade-95b9c6075c0b
- **Full PDF (Oct 2025):** https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099756111072525099/pdf/IDU-9eca2ce8-ced6-414a-9b1c-94554947d69a.pdf
- **April 2025 report (PDF):** https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/1b388949805c9a0ae3736bdacb32ea94-0050012025/original/CMO-April-2025.pdf
- **Forecasts table (PDF):** https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/55ed8b04df8228fea1607a97b5561f73-0050012025/related/CMO-October-2025-Forecasts.pdf
- **Key data points:**
  - Global commodity prices projected to fall to 6-year low in 2026 (4th consecutive year of decline)
  - Base metals index: ↑~2% in 2026–27
  - Copper and tin projected to reach new record highs (nominal USD)
  - Aluminum, nickel, tin, copper expected to see largest increases
  - Energy prices: ↓12% in 2025, ↓10% in 2026

### 5.5 RBA — The Global Energy Transition and Critical Minerals (October 2025)
- **Web:** https://www.rba.gov.au/publications/bulletin/2025/oct/the-global-energy-transition-and-critical-minerals.html
- **PDF:** https://www.rba.gov.au/publications/bulletin/2025/oct/pdf/the-global-energy-transition-and-critical-minerals.pdf
- **Authors:** Harry Stinson and Irene Cam
- **Key data points:**
  - Australia's critical minerals exports projected to reach ~10% of resource exports by 2030 (up from ~6%)
  - Late-stage lithium projects delayed in 2024 due to low prices
  - Nickel production declining (projects cancelled, production scaled back)
  - Lithium, rare earths, copper production expected to increase strongly over next 5 years

### 5.6 DISR — Resources and Energy Quarterly (December 2025)
- **Web:** https://www.industry.gov.au/publications/resources-and-energy-quarterly-december-2025
- **Covers:** Export value projections, production forecasts for key commodities
